Model interpretation

Positive coefficients increase expected log-ADT while negative ones decrease it. Posterior site multipliers reflect how much each location deviates from the network baseline after controlling for the selected features. Partial pooling stabilizes estimates for data-sparse short-term sites by pulling them toward the grand mean.

Interpretation

A 95% credible interval should contain ~95% of observations if the model is well calibrated. Continuous counters are close to nominal (≈95.6% at 95%). Short-term counters under-cover (≈68.6% at 95%), indicating the model intervals are too narrow for data-sparse sites.

Uncertainty reduction is measured as the % drop in average posterior predictive variance when a feature is retained versus removed. Larger values indicate a stronger contribution to narrowing the model's uncertainty. Direction comes from the sign of the standardized Bayesian ridge coefficient.
